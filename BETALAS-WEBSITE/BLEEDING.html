BLEEDING CONTROL

WHAT IS BLEEDING CONTROL?

Bleeding control, also known as hemorrhage control, is the immediate act of stopping or slowing down bleeding to prevent excessive blood loss, which can lead to shock, organ failure, or even death.

It is a vital first aid skill, especially in emergencies involving cuts, wounds, trauma, or accidents.

MORE INFORMATION

We have included a video for you to watch in order to gain a better understanding of how to do “Bleeding Control” or also known as “Hemorrhage Control”.

WHEN CAN I USE BLEEDING CONTROL?

You should use bleeding control techniques anytime someone is bleeding and the bleeding does not stop on its own — especially if the bleeding is severe, continuous, or life-threatening.
