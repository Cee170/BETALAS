WHAT IS THE PURPOSE OF
FIRST AID?

The purpose of first aid is to provide immediate and temporary care to a person who is injured or suddenly taken ill, before professional medical help is available.

The main goals can be summarized as the 3 Ps:

PRESERVE LIFE
PREVENT
FURTHER HARM
PROMOTE
RECOVERY

Cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR)

CPR use when a person is unconscious, not breathing, or not breathing normally (like gasping), and they have no pulse or show no signs of life.

Bleeding Control (Hemorrhage Control)

Bleeding control is often part of first aid, trauma care, or Stop the Bleed® training programs. If you're interested, I can guide you through simple techniques or recommend what supplies you should keep in a bleeding control kit.

Choking First Aid

Choking relief is used the moment someone shows signs of a blocked airway—especially if they can't speak, cough, or breathe. Acting quickly with back blows, abdominal thrusts, or CPR can save a life. Always call emergency services if the object isn’t cleared or if the person becomes unresponsive.d.

Burn First Aid

Burn first aid focuses on cooling the burn, protecting the skin, and preventing infection while monitoring for serious complications. For any burn that’s large, deep, on the face, hands, genitals, or over joints, always seek immediate medical attention.

Seizure First Aid

Seizure first aid is about staying calm, keeping the person safe, and not interfering with the seizure. Let it run its course, protect them from harm, and monitor them closely. With the right steps, you can provide meaningful help during what can be a frightening event.
