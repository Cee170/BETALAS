CPR

WHAT IS CPR?

The term "CPR" stands for "cardio" (heart), "pulmonary" (lungs), and "resuscitation" (restoring life). It involves two key actions: chest compressions and, if trained, rescue breaths.

Chest compressions are the most important part of CPR and are done by pushing hard and fast in the center of the person’s chest, ideally at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute and at a depth of about 2 inches.

WHEN CAN I USE CPR?

You should use CPR when a person is unconscious, not breathing, or not breathing normally (like gasping), and they have no pulse or show no signs of life.
