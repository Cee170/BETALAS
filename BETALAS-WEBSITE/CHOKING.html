CHOKING FIRST AID

WHAT IS CHOKING?

Choking happens when something becomes stuck in the throat or windpipe, blocking air from reaching the lungs.

The person may be unable to cough, speak, or breathe, and in severe cases, they may lose consciousness if the airway isn't cleared quickly.

Choking relief is the emergency first aid given to someone who is unable to breathe properly due to an object blocking their airway—usually food or a small object.

MORE INFORMATION

We have included a video for you to watch in order to gain a better understanding of how to do “Choking Relief”.

WHEN CAN I USE CHOKING RELIEF?

You should use choking relief immediately when you recognize that someone is choking and cannot breathe properly — especially if they cannot talk, cough, or make noise.
